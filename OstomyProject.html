Case Study

Ostomy Appliance Modification

October 2023 • Team project • AutoCAD • 3D printing

Low-fidelity prototype and test plan • patient-centred design

Summary

Project summary

We redesigned the connection between a two-piece ostomy system and the pouch to reduce skin pressure and leakage for a patient with a higher BMI. The solution separates the bag from direct skin contact using a short, rigid conduit and a flexible sealing ring to accommodate body contours while keeping the system manufacturable.

Role: Lead design & prototyping

Applicable Skills

  • Teamwork & Cooperation
  • Coordinating & Planning
  • AutoCAD Solid-modelling
  • 3D Printing & Prototyping

Project meta

AutoCAD 3D Printing Human Factors

Design Process

  1. Map user pain points and constraints (BMI, movement, materials).
  2. Sketch tunnel / conduit concepts to keep flow away from skin.
  3. 3D model and iterate on sealing geometry and interfaces.
  4. Produce low-fidelity 3D printed parts and run user tests.

Learnings

  • User comfort and ease-of-use trump theoretical performance in early prototypes.
  • Modular designs that allow quick seal swaps simplified testing and iteration.
  • Careful documentation of test posture & activity made results easier to compare.
